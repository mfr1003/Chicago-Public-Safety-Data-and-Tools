## Request

Pursuant to the Illinois Freedom of Information Act (5 ILCS 140), I respectfully
request the following records related to CTA fare evasion revenue loss and
enforcement-related expenditures.

### Record Set 1: Fare Evasion Loss Estimates

For each calendar year 2018 through present:

1. Any internal estimates, studies, audits, or analyses of annual revenue lost
   to fare evasion on the CTA rail system
2. Any internal estimates of fare evasion rates (percentage of riders not paying)
   by line, station, or system-wide
3. Any methodology documents describing how fare evasion loss is calculated or
   estimated
4. Any communications with the Regional Transportation Authority, Federal Transit
   Administration, or other agencies regarding fare evasion revenue loss estimates

Note: A CTA spokesperson was previously quoted stating it is "impossible to
accurately" measure fare evasion. If that assessment is documented internally,
records reflecting that determination are also responsive to this request.

### Record Set 2: Enforcement Infrastructure Costs

1. Cost of any fare gate upgrades, high-barrier gate installations, or gate
   modification projects undertaken from 2018 through present, including
   capital cost and ongoing maintenance cost
2. Any contracts with vendors for fare enforcement technology, including
   farecard inspection systems, turnstile sensors, or evasion detection tools
3. Any amounts paid by CTA to the Chicago Police Department for transit
   section services or reimbursements

### Record Set 3: Farecard Inspection Missions

Related to the farecard inspection program announced in March 2026:

1. Any policy, protocol, or operational document governing farecard inspection
   missions
2. Any records of farecard inspections conducted, including date, location,
   number of riders checked, and number of violations identified
3. Any cost estimates for the farecard inspection program
